I graduated from Northwestern University in December 2022, where I earned my Master of Science in Robotics. I graduated from Purdue University in May 2019, where I earned my B.S. in Mechanical engineering and The Certificate of Entrepreneurship and Innovation. After graduating, I gained hands on experience in robotic research and design at The Institute for Human & Machine Cognition (IHMC) in Pensacola, Florida, where I prototyped and assembled the legs and feet of the IHMC's very own hydraulic powered humanoid robot, NADIA. I also invented a haptic feedback system for rehabilitative exo-suits to relay leg and balance awareness, implimenting the device onto IHMC's QUIX exoskeleton.
